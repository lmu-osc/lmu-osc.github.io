# Reproducible Manuscripts

#####  Date & Time

25 Mar 2024  

#####  Location

Wikipedia Munich, Angertorstraße 3  

#####  Format

In-Person  

#####  Language

English  

  

Come along to talk about code, research tools, and open science in a social environment!

Possible resources to prepare your session’s hack:

Beginner:

- Quarto self-paced tutorial: <https://lmu-osc.github.io/introduction-to-Quarto/>
- [https://osf.io/mfe37](https://osf.io/mfe37) (10 min demonstration for executable manuscripts using RStudio/Rmd/Zenodo/Google doc)

Advanced:

- [https://plain-text.co/ ](https://plain-text.co/)
- [https://osf.io/ngd9t](https://osf.io/ngd9t) (10 min demonstration for executable manuscripts using Emacs/Sweave/Bib/Version control)

**Sessions’ agenda:**

- Brief round of introductions and description of the problems you’ll be working on during that session
- Formation of possible groups or tandems, if participants have tips to exchange on certain topics
- Hack
- Wrap-up: progress and next steps

Learn more about the Hacky Hour initiative [here](../../partners/local-os-initiatives/hacky-hour.llms.md "Hacky Hour").

*The original tutorial link pointed to Florian Pargent’s blog. However, the content for the Introduction to Quarto tutorial has since been migrated to the LMU Open Science Center’s GitHub Organization and domain.*
