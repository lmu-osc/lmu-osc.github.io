## Overview

The LMU Open Science Center is hosting its **5th hybrid Open Science Summer School**, offering participants the option to join **in person in Munich** or **online via Zoom**.

The summer school is designed to equip early career researchers and research support staff with practical Open Science skills through two complementary tracks.

### Track 1: Open Science knowledge and skills

**07–11 September 2026**

This track introduces participants to core Open Science practices aimed at improving the transparency, reproducibility, and credibility of research. It combines public lectures with hands-on workshops.

Participants will learn how to:

- Plan research designs and statistical analyses in advance (e.g. preregistration, data simulation)
- Build computationally reproducible workflows using version-controlled scripts
- Prepare and share data and code following FAIR principles and best practices

### Track 2: Open Science instructor training

**14–15 September 2026**

This track focuses on developing pedagogical and leadership skills for those interested in teaching and promoting Open Science. No prior teaching experience is required.

Participants will:

- Learn how to design and deliver Open Science workshops
- Apply effective teaching and engagement strategies
- Develop skills to advocate for Open Science within their communities
- Design and present their own workshop for future delivery

Participants are encouraged to implement their workshops after the program, with support from the LMU Open Science Center.
