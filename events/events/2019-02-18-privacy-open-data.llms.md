# Maintaining Privacy with Open Data

#####  Date & Time

18 Feb 2019  

#####  Location

  

#####  Format

In-Person  

#####  Language

German  

  

The OSC offers a workshop on data privacy and anonymity for participants ranging from graduate students to professors. The workhop will be held by Felix Schönbrodt and Anton Marx.

Contents:

- Why should we share data?
- Which kinds of research data need to be protected? What are personal data?
- How can the risk of re-identification be assessed?
- How can data be anonymized?
- A short discussion about new technological solutions that allow sharing sensible data (e.g. synthetical data, scientific use files, differential privacy)
- What are easy steps to enhance procedural safety in the use of sensible data?

The workshop contains of both theoretical input and hands-on exercises. Please bring your own laptop (preferably with R and RStudio installed). Skills in R will be beneficial but not essential.
