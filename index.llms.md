# Our Mission

### The researcher-led LMU Open Science Center (OSC) advances research excellence at LMU Munich by promoting open, responsible, and reproducible practices.

- ✓ Essential training in the highest standards of reproducible research methods
- ✓ Digital sovereignty and resilience to global challenges through open source workflows
- ✓ Transparency as foundation for trust and evidence-based societal debates
- ✓ Reliable scientific data as a critical foundation for responsible AI, digitalization, and societal translation

###### Hybrid · Jun 10, 2026

##### [OSPARK Bootcamp Munich - Promote your Open Research Passion!](events/events/2026-06-10-ospark-bootcamp.llms.md)

The \[OSPARK bootcamp\](https://oscars-project.eu/projects/ospark-bootcamp-open-science-promotion-and-advocacy-research-knowledge-bootcamp) is all about creating visibility through outreach and marketing. It's a learning opportunity for people working in or leading Open Research initiatives, services or infrastructures. The bootcamp focuses on the needs of Open Research ambassadors and provides ample space to exchange ideas and learn from one another. \*\*This bootcamp consists of a 6-week online course and a 2-day onsite workshop in Munich, Germany.\*\* \*\*Online course\*\* participants can expect: - Emails with information and tasks (including workbooks) every Tuesday and Thursday - One 15-minute check-in per week with another participant (your buddy) to exchange about problems and questions - A 1-hour online session per week with an expert who answers questions and facilitates the group discussion All in all, please expect to work about \*\*3 hours per week\*\* on the online course. You will spend much of that time on your initiative's outreach, not just on learning. At the 2-day \*\*onsite workshop\*\* we will tackle common challenges and work together on promoting the wonderful initiatives, services, and infrastructures you are building. \*\*Venue\*\* The onsite workshop will be held at LMU Munich. Details on the location will be provided to the participants. \### Important Dates - March 2026: Participation applications open - March 29, 2026: Early notifications of participation - May 10, 2026: General participation applications close - May 20, 2026: Notifications of participation for general applicants - June 10 – July 15: Online course - July 23 – July 25: Onsite workshop in Munich \[For more details, check out the event website.\](https://events.digital-research.academy/event/131/) \*\*Free participation:\*\* Funding for running the OSPARK bootcamp is provided by the \[OSCARS project\](https://oscars-project.eu/). \*\*Travel Stipends for Onsite Workshop\*\* We are able to offer a few stipends to support participants with travel and hotel costs in the range of \*\*€200–500\*\*. \*\*Inclusive Event\*\* We as organizers aim to make this event as inclusive as possible. Please let us know about your needs in the registration form. \*\*Research\*\* As a participant, you will be asked to take part in some research evaluating the course. More information will be provided closer to the time. \*\*Organizers\*\* The OSPARK Bootcamp is organized by the \[Digital Research Academy\](https://digital-research.academy/) and \[OLS\](https://we-are-ols.org/), funded via the \[OSCARS project\](https://oscars-project.eu/). The onsite workshop is held in collaboration with the LMU Open Science Center.

###### Hybrid · Sep 7, 2026

##### [LMU Open Science Summer School 2026](events/events/2026-09-07-lmu-open-science-summer-school.llms.md)

The LMU Open Science Center is hosting its \*\*5th hybrid Open Science Summer School\*\*, offering participants the option to join \*\*in person in Munich\*\* or \*\*online via Zoom\*\*. The summer school is designed to equip early career researchers and research support staff with practical Open Science skills through two complementary tracks. \*\*Apply or register now!\*\* \### Track 1: Open Science knowledge and skills \*\*07–11 September 2026\*\* This track introduces participants to core Open Science practices aimed at improving the transparency, reproducibility, and credibility of research. It combines public lectures with hands-on workshops. Participants will learn how to: - Plan research designs and statistical analyses in advance (e.g. preregistration, data simulation) - Build computationally reproducible workflows using version-controlled scripts - Prepare and share data and code following FAIR principles and best practices \### Track 2: Open Science instructor training \*\*14–15 September 2026\*\* This track focuses on developing pedagogical and leadership skills for those interested in teaching and promoting Open Science. No prior teaching experience is required. Participants will: - Learn how to design and deliver Open Science workshops - Apply effective teaching and engagement strategies - Develop skills to advocate for Open Science within their communities - Design and present their own workshop for future delivery Participants are encouraged to implement their workshops after the program, with support from the LMU Open Science Center. \### Funding & Partners The summer school is organized by LMU Open Science Center Train-the-Trainer program coordinator \[Dr. Sarah von Grebmer zu Wolfsthurn\](/people/people/sarah-von-grebmer-zu-wolfsthurn.qmd). The program is funded by the LMU Central Study Fund and the Volkswagen Foundation Pioneer Project grant, with in-kind support from the University Library of LMU Munich. Materials were developed in collaboration with \[PROFiL\](https://www.lmu.de/profil/de/) and \[FORRT\](https://forrt.org/). \![\](images/2026-09-07-lmu-open-science-summer-school-funders-logo.jpg){width=60%} For more information and full program, visit: \<http://lmu-osc.github.io/Open-Science-Summer-School-2026/\>

###### Past Event · In-Person · Mar 11, 2026

##### [Software Carpentry: Essential Computing Skill Training (Shell, Git, Python)](events/events/2026-03-11-software-carpentry-computing-skills.llms.md)

In this Software Carpentry Workshop you will learn the \*\*Unix Shell, version control in Git, and programming in Python\*\*. This \*\*two-day workshop\*\* teaches core computing skills that will help you be more productive and effective. We will alternate short tutorials with hands-on practical exercises. Participants will be encouraged to help one another and to apply what they have learned to their own research problems. Registration is mandatory, and participation requires setting up the software in advance by following the provided instructions (a help desk session will also take place on March 9). Please register only if you are available for the whole course. \*\*Primary target audience\*\*: biology students, (bio)medical students including those in dr. med. programs. The workshop is free and open to researchers, staff, and students of any discipline. It is funded by the Munich Center for Machine Learning and the LMU Open Science Center.
