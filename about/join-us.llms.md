# Join Us

### Becoming a member

#### Individual Members

Become a member of the OSC if you want to visibly support the goal of open, responsible, and reproducible research within your disciplines or at LMU Munich more broadly. As a member, you can

- shape our activities (e.g. suggest or lead an event, write research practice guidance, organize a meetup, liaise with stakeholders to inform on the design of incentives and policies)
- network with like-minded researchers across disciplines (from networking lunch to writing meta research proposals together), and
- vote and/or take part in the [OSC Scientific Board](../people/scientific-board.llms.md).

According to its [statute](../about/statutes.llms.md), all PhD holders (post docs, lecturers, PDs, and Professors) of LMU Munich as well as researchers from other universities or non-university research institutes contributing to foster the goals of the OSC can become members - free of any charge, of course.

[Become an OSC Member ](mailto:malika.ihle@lmu.de?subject=OSC%20Membership)

#### OSC Fellows

Become an OSC Fellow if you are a PhD candidate or a student from LMU Munich wanting to contribute to the OSC’s activities. For instance, you will be recognized with the status of OSC Fellow if you (co-)lead a grassroot initiatives, (co-)organize an event, and/or (co-)deliver a workshop, on your own or with OSC staff or members. The OSC strives to provide [OSC Fellows](../people/fellows.llms.md) with further leadership and training opportunities.

[Become an OSC Fellow ](mailto:malika.ihle@lmu.de?subject=OSC%20Fellowship)

#### Institutional Members

Departments, faculties, units, or central institutions of the LMU that provide infrastructure or other services that facilitate the goals of the OSC can become [institutional members](../partners/institutional-members.llms.md). Learn more about the benefits of becoming a funding institutional member below and schedule a call with us!
